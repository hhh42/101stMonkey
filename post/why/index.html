Motivation

Nov 21, 2014 · 2 minute read

At some point in many an engineers career they get lured into management. It’s often seen as a good thing and there are many benefits to it and for the most part I don’t regret the path. What I do miss is the making of things. The actual stacking of the blocks. The satisfaction of seeing the nicely stack of freshly chopped wood. It’s a well documented thing that building something with your hands is satisfying. Coding, though a bit more abstract, does have a similar satisfaction. Seeing something go that you wrote has a similar feel. Not as rewarding but again more than you would think are unit tests and the like.

Great Video on what motivates us and the improvement of ones abilities and knowledge. I absolutly felt that pull into the field of computers. It was a very clean way to make things. When I first got pulled into managment I didn’t continue coding enough to scratch the itch and became unsettled by it. I had a novel idea for a game engine to “fix” some of the micro managing nature of the early command and conquer style games that were just becoming popular in the late 90s. So I went off to learn about graphics and automated planning and backend technologies.

I’ve had several stack changes over the years playing with these ideas. I had settled on a Google App Engine based stack before I found Scala, which caused me to rethink several things thought libGDX remained constant. LibGDX doesn’t seem to care to much (though the need for source for the GWT compile has by stymied for a but though there are some solutions in the works, I hope to not solve that problem and let others)

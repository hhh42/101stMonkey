Sysadmin

Dec 1, 2014 · 2 minute read

Tools

For many years I never bothered to learn anything about being an effective admin. I rarely needed those skills and a would fumble my way through whatever the need was at the time.

Using a detachable terminal like screen or Byobu is a nescessity when managing remote servers. The Listen to Mozart while learning Byobo is a pleasant over view of what it can do if you haven’t tried. For me this replaced many of my lame tricks and I’ve still know about half the keybinding I should. If your still hacking away at a “classic” terminal, do youself a favor and upgrade. I won’t tell you how long it took me to upgrade.

What is going on?

Processors

How often have you sat at a prompt wondering what the computer is doing. The above has some rudimentary monitoring but often you need more. Top is a good start htop is better. Not as profound an upgrade as byobu is but still work a bump

Networking

As fun as vmstat 1 is you don’t get the actual picture, sure you know the lights are on but you have no idea what’s going on in the room.

Nethogs

For a good sense of the WHO of network usage nethogs is a quick way to see.

iptraf

For a quick look at the WHAT of network usage iptraf does the trick. Though not as big a beast as wireshare it seems to answer most of the questions I see to have. Granted I’m no networking guy but for getting a sense if things are working etc. it seems to do the trick

Key Exchange

This is another thing I took way to long to mature at. Yeah, I typed passwords longer than I should. Do yourself a favor and setup keys

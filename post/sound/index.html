Linux Sound

Dec 1, 2014 · 2 minute read

Linux handling of sound hardware/software has been nothing short of terrible. I think it’s been a huge contributer to delaying desktop adoption of Linux and a huge personal annoyance.

As a non professional musican I want to do the basic use cases. Do the basic 4 track exercises. Record a few tracks until I like them. Basic mixing. Basic effects. Setting up play along and practice tracks. Nothing profound.

As an audio snob I want good sounding intruments. I’m not going to tolerate anything but an excellent piano sound. A reverb that sounds like your in a toilet paper roll isn’t going to fly either.

Note app let me down

Apparently I can’t scroll like you think in that app.

VST stack

The easiest way I have found to get a decent sound stack working is to first add the kxstudio repositoroies. At that point you can just

”’ apt-get install cadence dssi-vst low-latency-linux “’

and I get pretty much what I need. Need to test out the kxstudio defaults.. That apparently changes some important settings.

the kxstudio repositories seem to have vastly more recent things all working together than other distro’s I’ve changed.

Once all the above installs and you reboot. A bit of fiddling in the jack config and things seem to work out. Cadence provides a good way to manage bridges etc if you need that. I’ve only used the ALSA one a few times.

Catia

This is certinly my goto app for begining to diagnose. I certibnly prefer that to the grid interface of Ardour and since it’s all jack it works out no matter where you change it.

VSTHost

seems to work better for me than festige did. It could be just underlying versions of things but immediatly it seemed to respond better, load faster and I found a plugin that the images worked in while they didn’t using festige. I haven’t tried to save or load state yet so I’ll have to reserve judgement

Session Management

I’ve seen many solutions out there and some might work for some people but I haven’t found something that really works for me. My mostly lame mostly works solution is to load all the VSTs I might need up front then load Ardour which wires up most everything provided it touches Ardour.. which may or may not be a good practice but it’s something doesn’t limit me much in my workflow so I generally do that.

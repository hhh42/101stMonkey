Getting Started

Nov 20, 2014 · 2 minute read

So you want to blog?

Well more like I need to do some writing. I wanted something simple something I didn’t have to fight. I just want a place to enter some text and it should look nice without some hassle making or hosting it. This of course leads you to find all these fancy static site generators. I was recommended Octopress and quickly found lots of text that made it sounds all very wonderful. I liked the range of templating capabilites. Seemed to be designed for hackers etc… All good on paper and for many clearly capable.

Jekyll was my second choice and this was built on it so it must be wonderful, and I’m sure it is. I wouldn’t know. Let’s just say my first experience with Ruby wasn’t a pleasant one. I’m the kind of guy that makes fun of Ruby. I think this guy has the right of it.

For some inexplicable reason I couldn’t get everything to install. Granted I didn’t try in any real “pull up the sleaves” kind of way, but the point of this project was to bet quick and easy. I made a few stabs, tried a few different distro’s. I got various inscrutible errors that didn’t result much from google. Let’s go play with RVM yeah, that’ll be fun because what you need from your toy langauges is multiple versions “managed”. Aren’t toy languages supposed to be easy and fun to setup? Isn’t that half the appeal, low overhead and maintenance? Aren’t the Ruby folk all “We don’t have these kinds of problems like the PHP folk!”.

Anyway, I should probably save bashing Ruby and other toy languages for a more in depth focused series of comments but for now I’d like to thank their gross inadequacies and the fine correlative capabilites of staticgen because I started from the top and went until I found a real language. Ohh look Go is a real language! I think I’ll try Hugo and sure enough within a few short minutes everything was working and I was picking themes.

Something tells me Hugo will be a tad faster than a Ruby Hello world…. so far I haven’t seen it go over 20ms.
